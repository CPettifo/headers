Your Request Headers Tell a Story

A good amount of intformation is given away in a request header, which includes details like your browser, operating system, language settings, IP address, and other metadata. This information is normally used to optimise functionality and user experience (for example serving region or language-specific content), but it can also reveal personal data about you. Your headers can be used to link your browsing habits to you even if you block trackers, reject cookies, and use a VPN!

Cover Your Tracks does a much better job of explaining this, and has a free tool for testing how identifiable you are in your current browser.
